Speculative Installation Design
— Breath of Europa

Project Description

As a speculative inquiry into extreme environments, the project imagines how humans might one day live on Europa. I developed a large-scale architectural installation intended to support survival both on the surface and beneath the ice—where a global ocean is believed to exist. Informed by research on ice composition and geothermal patterns, the design integrates under-ice water electrolysis for oxygen production with solar energy systems. Through sketches, renderings, and prototyping, Breath of Europa offers a poetic reflection on human adaptability and coexistence with alien environments.

Speculative Installation Design
— Breath of Europa

Design Process

Research on Europa's real environmental conditions was conducted, including analysis of current space technologies, extreme-environment life-support systems, ice composition, and geothermal activity. The design proposes a composite structural system that couples subglacial water electrolysis for in-situ oxygen generation with solar energy harvesting. Deliverables include high-fidelity visualizations, parametric/digital models, and a working physical prototype.

Outcome

A functional, interactive physical installation, complemented by photorealistic renderings and an animated demonstration video that situate the proposal within a plausible living scenario.

Product Design
— Top-proof Outdoor Gas Stove

Project Description

Inspired by the challenges of outdoor cooking on uneven terrain, I designed an adjustable, portable gas stove engineered to remain stable on rocky ground. The design balances both functionality and lightness, solving the issue of tipping while keeping the product easy to carry. Through user interviews and product analysis, I studied how outdoor enthusiasts interact with existing stoves and identified their frustrations. Iterating from sketches to 3D models, I refined the structure and added intuitive visual cues. The final concept achieves both precision in function and clarity in form, offering a safer and more user-centered outdoor experience.

Product Design
— Top-proof Outdoor Gas Stove

Design Process

In-depth user research was conducted with outdoor cooking enthusiasts, along with competitive product analysis to identify current strengths and weaknesses. Through brainstorming and iterative prototyping, new product concepts were developed, tested and refined into final 3D models and high-fidelity renderings.

Outcome

A comprehensive package including user-research documentation, design sketches, and contextualized digital renderings that demonstrate the stove's stability and usability in real outdoor scenarios.

Interactive Automata Design
— Thousand-hand Guanyin Automata

Project Description

Inspired by the Thousand-hand Guanyin mural from Dunhuang, this project designs an interactive puzzle toy. It applies principles of human–computer interaction (HCI) to heighten sensory engagement and emotional resonance, offering users a fresh lens on Dunhuang culture. Developed from precise AutoCAD drawings to hand assembly, the piece captures Guanyin's spiritual poise while reframing cultural heritage through contemporary mechanical curiosity.

Interactive Automata Design
— Thousand-hand Guanyin Automata

Design Process

The mechanism creatively integrates a "shutter structure" to achieve multi-directional motion and a planetary gear system for coordinated transmission. More than 20 motion tests were conducted to refine the structure and tolerances. Precise components were detailed in AutoCAD, then fabricated, hand-finished, and assembled into a working prototype.

Outcome

A fully functional kinetic puppet installation was completed, accompanied by high-fidelity renderings and an animated demonstration video to showcase motion logic and interaction.

Emotion-soothing Interactive Light Installation
— Diphylleia Luminaire

Project Description

Inspired by Diphylleia, whose leaves become transparent upon contact with water, this project reinterprets a subtle natural phenomenon into a lighting installation that responds to moisture, evoking calmness and emotional healing in public spaces. By analyzing the plant's biological properties and experimenting with multiple transparency materials, I developed a biomimetic system that transforms natural behaviors into interactive light and texture. The result is a poetic, nature-driven installation that connects quiet environmental shifts with human feelings.

Emotion-soothing Interactive Light Installation
— Diphylleia Luminaire

Design Process

Based on research into the plant's morphology and transparency mechanism, the design focuses on reproducing the petal/leaf transformation that occurs when exposed to water. Five candidate materials were tested for water-induced translucency, followed by the development of a biomimetic structural system. Dynamic renderings and physical prototype models were produced to simulate how the installation responds to both environmental moisture and human presence.

Outcome

A set of installation models, visual renderings, and an interactive video presentation demonstrating how the device engages with its surroundings and audiences.
